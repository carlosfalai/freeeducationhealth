Patients & families

A health helper for your household.

You are setting up a free health-preparation chatbot on Telegram for yourself, your family, or your community. It asks structured questions about a symptom, has at least two independent AI models answer and cross-check each other, and gives back plain-language considerations and next steps. It is not a doctor and never pretends to be one.

Everything runs on your own computer with your own accounts. Nobody else — including the people who wrote this — can see what you or your family type. You arrive at whatever medical care you can reach with your story already organized.

What you need

  • A computer that stays on while the bot is in use (any laptop works).
  • A free Telegram bot token — Telegram's own @BotFather gives you one in two minutes.
  • Access to at least 2 AI models: two API keys (for example Anthropic, DeepSeek, OpenAI, a small pay-as-you-go cost), or a free local model via Ollama.

Set it up in three steps

1

Download your piece (only this one)

Get the patients-and-families bot from the download page and unzip it. That single file contains the bot and everything it needs. You do not need anything else from this project.

2

Install a free AI assistant app (recommended)

The easiest way to set this up is to let an AI assistant do it for you. No coding is needed on your part: you install the app, then ask it in plain words to set up the bot and it reads the runbook inside the folder you just unzipped. This project was built and tested with Claude Code, but any capable assistant works.

# Windows (PowerShell) irm https://claude.ai/install.ps1 | iex # macOS / Linux curl -fsSL https://claude.ai/install.sh | bash # Or, if you already use Node.js npm install -g @anthropic-ai/claude-code

Then open a terminal inside the unzipped folder, start the assistant, and ask it to set up the Telegram bot and walk you through getting your bot token and AI keys. It asks you only for the things that must be yours.

3

Or run the setup wizard yourself

You do not strictly need an assistant. Open a terminal in the unzipped folder and run the wizard:

node bot/setup.cjs

It is a friendly question-and-answer wizard. It helps you connect your own keys for two or more different AIs (it suggests OpenAI/ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and DeepSeek, with the link to get each), typed in hidden so they never show on screen. It writes your settings file for you and never sends anything anywhere. Then accept the consent notice and start the bot:

node bot/accept-consent.cjs node bot/index.cjs

Prefer editing files by hand? SETUP.md section "Setting up the Telegram bot (patients)" and bot/README.md cover that too.

Before you rely on it: this is not a doctor, and not emergency care. Every session starts with a banner saying so, and that emergencies go to real care immediately. That banner cannot be turned off.
  • Don't type names, phone numbers, or ID numbers. The bot filters likely identifiers out of what it sends to the AI providers, but the filter is best-effort, and the habit is the real protection.
  • Whoever runs the bot (you) can see what people send it. Only give your bot's address to people who know and accept that.
  • Be careful with anyone online claiming to be a licensed physician while just passing along AI output. Many places publish a free public register where you can verify a doctor's license by name.
