Published method · zero infrastructure

The template method

The simplest published method, and the ancestor of all the others: talk, transcribe, then have any capable chatbot fill your strict clinical templates. Nothing to install, nothing to host. The intelligence is not the chatbot — it is the template discipline, refined case after case in a working practice and published here in full.

The flow

1

Capture

During or after the consult, record or dictate the case. A chatbot's voice mode records, transcribes, and keeps the case in the conversation; any transcription system works just as well — the point is that all the talk becomes text.

2

Discuss

Reason about the case in the same chat: the differential, what to rule out, the plan, what you intend to prescribe.

3

Format

Paste the template pack below and ask for the documents you need — SOAP note, prescriptions, labs, referral, imaging requisition, work leave, the case summary, the timeline, the decision flow, and the patient explanation paragraph. The model fills your structures; it is never allowed to freestyle.

4

Review and use

Every output is a draft. You review, correct, and only then use it. Nothing is ever sent to anyone by a machine.

Why so strict? Free-form AI output drifts: it wraps text in boxes, decorates with bold, mixes an example with the current patient, restates the wrong case. Every rule in the pack exists because that failure happened at least once. The two load-bearing disciplines: context control (the filled examples are format only — the model applies the structure to the current case, never the example's content) and formatting bans (no wrapping, no bold, no dashes as punctuation, labs one per line, patient-facing text in the patient's language, clinical documents never referencing the messaging medium).

Privacy rules for this method

A general chatbot is a consumer service. What you paste travels to that provider under consumer terms, not a healthcare agreement. Work on case numbers, never names or identifying numbers — identity stays in your own chart system. If you must process identifiable data, use a BAA-covered route instead (see the form-robot page). Turn off chat history/training options where offered, and treat the transcript as clinical scratch paper — it is not the chart.

The template pack — one click, then paste it into your chatbot

All filled examples inside are fabricated format references — no real patient appears. Adapt the wording, language, and letterhead to your practice and jurisdiction.

IMPORTANT CONTEXT CONTROL
The case we are working on is the last patient discussed immediately
before this message. Any SOAP / prescription / referral / imaging
templates below are FORMAT EXAMPLES ONLY.
DO NOT reuse the demographics, diagnosis, or treatment from the examples.
DO NOT mix cases. DO NOT summarize or restate the example case.
Apply the example template structure, populated only with data from the
current case. If ambiguous, default to the most recently discussed
patient and plan, not the example.
FORMATTING, ALWAYS: no code-block wrapping, no bold, no dashes as
punctuation, no extra commentary around the documents. Patient-facing
text is written in the patient's own language. Clinical documents never
mention the messaging medium.

CASE SUMMARY FORMAT (one paragraph, fixed opening "Summary:", fabricated
example):
Summary: A 32-year-old pregnant woman at 8.6 weeks gestation presents
with vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain (7/10), fatigue, and night sweats.
The clinical picture is consistent with threatened miscarriage, but
ectopic pregnancy and other serious first-trimester complications must
be excluded urgently. Immediate transvaginal ultrasound and hCG
quantification are essential to determine fetal viability and guide
management.
Optionally follow with a one-line thesis, e.g.: The X-ray confirms what
is broken, but not what could be permanently damaged, and those
unanswered questions are exactly why in-person assessment is mandatory.

HORIZONTAL ASCII TIMELINE: a horizontal timeline of the illness from
symptom onset to today, exact dates, Day-N counts at each event, text
symbols for onset, trigger, worsening, improvement, and today.

ASCII DECISION FLOW: the diagnostic logic as a vertical flow —
presenting problem; what is known (check marks); what is unknown; what
must be ruled out and the consequence of missing it; the decision
threshold; the resulting action. Mirror how the case was actually
reasoned, including what an X-ray or test can and cannot answer.

PATIENT EXPLANATION PARAGRAPH ("#7") — RULES:
One single warm, natural paragraph in the patient's own language, placed
at the very top of the reply, never wrapped in a box, no bullets, no
lists, no dashes. It opens with what the physician believes is going on;
names each treatment proposed, what it is, how it works, how it is
taken, which side effects to watch for and how to minimize them; uses
the future tense (je vais vous prescrire), never the past; never gives
generalized promises like "I'll prescribe you treatment"; draws only on
the plan already discussed in this case (sections 0 to 7 of the
discussion). Telemedicine rules: never offer an in-person appointment;
if in-person evaluation is needed, say a referral note will be provided
so the patient does not have to re-explain everything (a doctor-to-doctor
document, already organized), note the patient arranges that visit
themselves, and offer 1 to 3 days of work leave so they can arrange it
without pressure. Never end with promises of follow-up.

DeckStation — one button per template, free

Pasting the same templates dozens of times a day gets old. DeckStation is a small, free Windows desktop app from this project — an always-on-top dock of buttons that floats above every window, like Stream Deck software without the hardware. Each button can paste text with one click: load the template pack into its buttons once, and formatting a case becomes press-SOAP, press-Prescription, press-Referral — straight into whatever chatbot window you are working in. A pre-loaded profile with the clinical template pack ships with it.

Honesty about its condition: DeckStation works, and it is offered free — but it has rough edges and real bugs. It is provided source-included precisely so you can fix what bothers you: open its folder in an AI coding assistant (built and tested with Claude Code; any capable one works) and describe what broke — "the dock stopped staying on top, fix it" — and let the assistant repair your copy. That is not a workaround; it is the intended way to own software like this.

The free bundle is being finalized after a full pre-publication security audit and will appear on the download page with the template profile already inside.
