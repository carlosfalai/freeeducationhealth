Manifesto — world edition

If you are far from a doctor, this was built first for you.

In much of the world there is one physician for tens of thousands of people. The clinic is a day's travel away, the visit costs a week's wages, and no appointment is ever coming. This project puts its first users first: not the comfortable, but the far.

The cost that changes everything

Here is the fact this whole project rests on. A structured health consultation — the careful questions a clinician would ask, checked by more than one independent AI — costs on the order of a few cents of computing. Not a few dollars. Cents.

The knowledge is written. The models exist. The cost is pocket change. That it still is not in the hands of the people who need it most is no longer a resource problem; it is a choice. This project is a refusal to keep making it.

We do not promise a doctor in a box. No software is a physician. What we promise is smaller and real: that a family far from care can think clearly about a health problem, know the warning signs, and arrive prepared at whatever care exists.

One shared phone is enough

You do not need a computer in every home. One ordinary smartphone, shared by a family or a village, connected to a Telegram bot that a local organizer runs, gives everyone who can reach that phone:

  • A structured interview in plain language — the same questions a careful clinician would ask, so the problem is described properly the first time.
  • Clear warning signs for this specific problem: what can be watched at home, what should not wait, and when the journey to care is worth making now.
  • An answer cross-checked by more than one independent AI, with disagreement shown rather than hidden.
  • A record that stays with the family, not on someone else's server.

A one-page summary that travels

Every consultation ends in a portable, one-page summary the patient keeps: the history, the timeline, the questions already answered, the signs already checked. It travels with the patient to whatever care exists — the traveling clinic that comes through monthly, the community health worker two villages over, the distant hospital at the end of a long trip.

Minutes with a clinician are precious when they are rare. A patient who arrives with a clear, complete story gets more out of every one of those minutes — and nothing about their history is lost between visits, because they carry it themselves.

Guidance that never assumes a doctor is near

Most health tools quietly assume a clinic around the corner. This one does not. For every problem it holds to the same honest frame:

What to do

Sensible, non-drug care that is safe at home: rest, fluids, wound cleaning, fever comfort, watching and recording — the things that help while you decide.

What to watch

The specific signs for this problem that mean it is getting worse — so a family is not guessing in the dark at two in the morning.

When to travel

An honest read of urgency: what can wait for the traveling clinic, what should go to the health worker this week, and what is worth the long trip today.

In many of these regions, a pharmacy or a self-pay laboratory is reachable long before a physician is. The tool respects that reality: it can explain what kinds of tests a clinician would typically consider for a problem, so a visit to a lab or a health worker is better spent. What it will never do is name a medicine to go and buy. Antibiotics chosen by guesswork are one of the documented drivers of drug resistance, and drug resistance falls hardest on exactly these communities. Medicines belong in a trained hand — the tool helps you reach one prepared.

One organizer can carry a community

This is deliberately built so that one person — a teacher, a community health worker, a shop owner with a laptop, anyone patient enough to follow instructions — can run a node for everyone around them.

1

Set it up once

Download the free, open-source system onto one computer and connect it to a Telegram bot. The setup guide assumes no technical background.

2

Share the bot

Anyone who can message that bot — from any phone, in their own language — can start a structured consultation. One node serves a family, a street, a village.

3

Help it spread

Everything is free to copy. When a neighboring community wants its own node, hand them the same files. No permission, no license fee, no center that can fail.

Nothing here depends on us.

The whole system — code, guides, this website — can be downloaded, copied, and re-hosted by anyone. If this project disappeared tomorrow, every running node would keep working, and any of them could start new ones. That is the point: health guidance so widely held that no one can withhold it.

The honest limits

Read this part slowly. This is software, not a physician, and it is not emergency care. It will not diagnose you with certainty, it will never tell you to medicate yourself, and it will never name an antibiotic to buy — self-medication drives drug resistance and can cost lives. Where a clinician is reachable, see the clinician. What this tool offers, where no help is coming, is the difference between facing a health problem with structure and facing it with a guess.

A few cents of computing, in the hands that need it most.
