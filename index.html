Free health help you run yourself.

Your family describes symptoms. Several different AIs check each other's answers. You get plain-language information and a one-page summary you can bring to a real doctor. It runs on your own computer, so there is no company in the middle holding your family's health questions.

Find your starting point See how it works

Not one AI. A panel that has to agree.

An ordinary health chatbot is one AI answering alone, and whatever it gets wrong goes straight to you. This is different: every answer is cross-checked by two or more different AIs — for example ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and DeepSeek.

  • The same symptom answers go to each AI separately.
  • They have to agree before you are shown anything.
  • When they disagree in a way that matters, it tells you — it never hides one AI's confident guess.
  • If any single AI flags a warning sign, that warning is kept.
  • If too few of them answer properly, it refuses to answer rather than falling back to just one.

This cross-check is the biggest difference from an ordinary health chatbot — a built-in second and third opinion. See exactly how it works →

Everyone runs their own copy

The word we use for this is "healthcare blockchain," but there are no coins and nothing to buy. It borrows one idea from Bitcoin: instead of one company running one service for everyone, every person keeps their own complete copy of the software — like owning your own cookbook instead of depending on a single restaurant.

Once you have downloaded it, it is yours and it keeps working no matter what happens to any company, server, or even to this project. That is the whole point: health help that cannot be quietly switched off, and that you can pass to family the way you'd copy a file. Read the idea in plain words →
